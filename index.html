InkWorld 1.0

A Chinese Painting World Model with Long-Horizon Consistency

Abstract

Traditional Chinese painting represents a unique visual expression system, characterized by a scattered perspective and compositional spatial organization, which often differs from the geometric assumptions of real-world scenes. Recent advances in generative video models have enabled Chinese paintings to come alive as dynamic, controllable visual content, a capability that is increasingly in demand. However, existing approaches often suffer from consistency issues, stemming from the lack of appropriate modeling of the underlying compositional structure and perspective principles in Chinese paintings. To this end, we propose InkWorld, a world model for consistent video generation for Chinese painting. Our key idea is to explicitly incorporate layer-wise compositional structures into the learning process without introducing additional architectural complexity. To achieve this, we decompose each training video into multiple semantic layers with both static structures and dynamic elements, and translate this decomposition into spatial weighting maps in the latent space. Based on this design, we introduce a layer-aware loss stratification mechanism, which reweights the velocity prediction objective to emphasize structurally stable regions and preserves dynamic expressiveness through mean normalization. In addition, we develop a unified control framework to jointly model camera motion and scene dynamics. Camera trajectories are represented using Plücker coordinates and integrated into Transformer attention, while motion-aware conditioning enables fine-grained temporal control across regions with varying dynamics. Our approach is model-agnostic and can be integrated into existing diffusion or flow-based video generation frameworks. The experimental analysis demonstrates that InkWorld effectively improves consistency for dynamic Chinese painting generation, providing a principled solution for modeling such a visual expression system.

Brief Overview

InkWorld 1.0 demonstrates consistent and controllable video generation for traditional Chinese paintings. By modeling layer-wise compositional structures and camera trajectories, our approach maintains long-horizon consistency in both static landscapes and dynamic elements.

Layer-Stratified Consistency Loss

The proposed Layer-Stratified Consistency Loss (LSCL) leverages the Layer Curation Pipeline to extract structured priors, and constructs an adaptive weighting mask formed by the multiplicative combination of three independent components.

Standard MSE

InkWorld

InkWorld combines a rectified-flow video backbone with explicit camera and motion conditioning, together with a layer-stratified consistency loss. The conditional pathway addresses controllable viewpoint traversal, while the weighted objective anchors stable semantic layers during long-horizon generation.
